⭐ Distinction-Level Behaviours

  • My proposal fully decomposes all sub-problems with inputs, processes, and outputs
  • Every risk in my proposal has a specific, named technical mitigation (not vague)
  • My FRs are verifiable (you can test whether they are met)
  • My NFRs are measurable (specific numbers, not "fast" or "easy")
  • My KPIs are business-relevant and quantifiable
  • My wireframes are annotated with HCI principles and accessibility notes
  • My algorithms handle edge cases and have a clear stopping condition for any recursion
  • My test log covers normal, erroneous, AND boundary data for each component
  • My test log includes regression tests after every fix
  • My code uses consistent naming conventions throughout (e.g. always camelCase or always snake_case)
  • All functions/methods have PHPDoc or JSDoc comments
  • My code has no global variables where local variables would work
  • My solution implements 6+ WCAG 2.1 AA success criteria
  • My error messages are specific and describe how to fix the problem
  • My evaluation references specific evidence from feedback (not just "users liked it")
  • My rationale for future iterations is specific and linked to measured problems
  • My documentation uses technical language consistently and appropriately for each audience
  • I have documented at least 3 iterations with rationale for each design decision

⚖️ Legislation Quick Reference

  • GDPR (2018): Data protection law — lawful basis for processing, consent, right to erasure, privacy by design, breach notification within 72 hours
  • Computer Misuse Act 1990: Three offences — unauthorised access, unauthorised access with intent, unauthorised modification
  • Equality Act 2010: Prohibits disability discrimination — requires reasonable adjustments for accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)
  • WCAG 2.1: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines — POUR principles, Level A/AA/AAA compliance
  • Copyright, Designs & Patents Act 1988: Governs use of third-party code, images, and media in your solution
  • Consumer Rights Act 2015: Software must be fit for purpose, as described, and of satisfactory quality
