👥 Digital Team Roles

Who Does What in a Digital Team

Product Owner, Scrum Master, Technical Lead, and the full cast of a modern software development team — responsibilities, interactions, and exam-ready definitions.

🎯
Where this appears in your OSP: Your proposal should reference relevant professional roles when describing your project context. Your evaluation should mention who would be involved in different phases if this were a real professional project. Understanding roles also helps you justify why you made certain decisions in your solo project.

🎯 Core Agile Roles

📋

Product Owner

Agile / Scrum
Avg UK salary:
£55,000–£80,000

The Product Owner (PO) is the single point of accountability for the product. They represent the voice of the customer and business stakeholders, translating their needs into actionable development work. They own the Product Backlog — the ordered list of all work to be done.

Crucially, the PO makes prioritisation decisions: deciding which features provide the most business value and should be built first. They are not a manager — they cannot tell the development team how to build things, only what to build and in what order.

  • Define and prioritise the Product Backlog
  • Write and refine User Stories with acceptance criteria
  • Attend Sprint Reviews and accept/reject completed work
  • Engage with stakeholders and translate needs to requirements
  • Make scope decisions within each sprint
  • Define KPIs and success metrics for the product
  • Maximise the value delivered by the development team
  • Communicate product vision to the whole team
💡
In your OSP: You are acting as both developer AND a form of Product Owner — you define requirements, prioritise features, and accept/reject your own work. Reference this in your proposal: "In a professional context, a dedicated Product Owner would manage stakeholder expectations and backlog prioritisation — in this solo project I have assumed this responsibility."
🛡️

Scrum Master

Agile / Scrum
Avg UK salary:
£50,000–£75,000

The Scrum Master is a servant-leader — they don't manage the team but serve them by removing obstacles (impediments) and ensuring the team can work at their best. They are guardians of the Scrum process and help the team continuously improve.

The Scrum Master is not a project manager — they don't assign tasks, track individual performance, or hold direct authority. Their power is facilitative. A good Scrum Master makes themselves less needed over time as the team becomes self-organising.

  • Facilitate Sprint Planning, Daily Stand-ups, Reviews, Retrospectives
  • Remove blockers and impediments for the team
  • Coach the team on Scrum practices and Agile values
  • Protect the team from external interruptions mid-sprint
  • Help the Product Owner refine the backlog
  • Foster a culture of continuous improvement
  • Track sprint velocity and help with capacity planning
  • Escalate unresolvable impediments to management
🛠️

Technical Lead

Engineering
Avg UK salary:
£65,000–£95,000

The Technical Lead (Tech Lead) is the most senior technical person on the team. They are responsible for technical decisions — choosing the technology stack, defining coding standards, reviewing architecture, and ensuring the codebase is maintainable and secure. They typically still write code but also mentor junior developers.

Unlike a manager, the Tech Lead's authority comes from technical expertise and trust. They are the person the team asks: "How should we build this?"

  • Define technical architecture and technology choices
  • Establish and enforce coding standards and conventions
  • Lead code reviews — approving pull requests
  • Identify and manage technical debt
  • Mentor junior and mid-level developers
  • Make security and performance architectural decisions
  • Work with PO to assess technical feasibility of requirements
  • Manage third-party integrations and dependencies
💡
In your OSP: Decisions you document — "I chose PHP over Python because the college hosting environment supports PHP natively" or "I selected bcrypt cost factor 12 to balance security and performance" — are exactly the type of decisions a Tech Lead makes. Frame them this way in your evaluation.

👤 Other Key Digital Roles

💻

Full-Stack Developer

Works on both front-end (HTML/CSS/JS) and back-end (PHP/Python/Node). Must understand the full request-response cycle. The role your OSP most closely represents.

UK avg: £45,000–£70,000

🎨

UX/UI Designer

UX (User Experience) — researches user needs, creates wireframes, runs usability tests. UI (User Interface) — designs the visual layer. Distinct from development but must collaborate closely.

UK avg: £35,000–£60,000

🧪

QA Engineer

Quality Assurance — designs test plans, executes manual and automated tests, reports defects, verifies fixes. Works closely with developers. Distinct from Developers who test their own code.

UK avg: £30,000–£55,000

🔐

Security Analyst

Identifies vulnerabilities, conducts penetration testing, ensures OWASP compliance, responds to security incidents. Increasingly embedded in dev teams (DevSecOps) rather than siloed.

UK avg: £45,000–£75,000

☁️

DevOps Engineer

Bridges development and operations. Manages CI/CD pipelines, cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure), containerisation (Docker/Kubernetes), monitoring, and deployment automation.

UK avg: £55,000–£85,000

📊

Data Analyst / Engineer

Collects, processes and analyses data to inform product decisions. Works with SQL, Python/R, and BI tools (Tableau, Power BI). Increasingly important as data-driven decisions become standard.

UK avg: £35,000–£60,000

🧠 Quick-Fire Definitions

What is the key difference between Product Owner and Project Manager?▼
A Project Manager focuses on delivery — time, budget, resources, risk. They manage how the project is delivered. A Product Owner focuses on value — what to build and why. They define what is delivered. In Scrum, traditional project management is distributed: the Scrum Master handles process, the PO handles priorities, the team handles how.
Can the Scrum Master and Product Owner be the same person?▼
No — this is explicitly prohibited in the Scrum Guide. The Scrum Master protects the team's capacity; the PO pushes for more features. These are conflicting interests. Combining the roles creates an inherent conflict of interest that undermines the process.
What is a "definition of done" and who sets it?▼
The Definition of Done (DoD) is a shared agreement within the Scrum Team about what criteria a user story must meet before it can be called complete. It's created collaboratively by the whole team (developers, QA, PO) and typically includes: code written, unit tests passing, code reviewed, acceptance criteria met, no known defects, documentation updated. The Scrum Master facilitates creating it; the Tech Lead often defines the technical criteria.
What does a Technical Lead do that a senior developer doesn't?▼
A Technical Lead has an additional leadership and communication dimension. Beyond writing code: they represent the team in architectural discussions with stakeholders, make technology stack decisions with long-term implications, are accountable for the overall code quality of the team's output, and actively mentor others. A senior developer excels technically; a Tech Lead enables the entire team to excel technically.
