🚀 Emerging Technologies

AI, IoT, ML, Blockchain, Drones & 5G

Understand the impact of emerging technologies on software development and society. The OSP mark scheme expects you to reference current and emerging technology in your proposal's wider context.

🎯
Where this appears in your OSP: Task 1A "Appreciation of Wider Issues" (9 marks) expects reference to "current practice and emerging tech in the [industry] context." The mark scheme sample specifically mentions: Apps, mobile devices, use of wearables, video/remote instruction. Distinction requires you to discuss how these technologies affect your solution decisions.
🤖

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Systems that perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence

📌 High Exam Relevance

AI encompasses systems that can reason, learn, and make decisions. In software development, AI tools now assist with code generation (GitHub Copilot), automated testing, bug detection, and requirement analysis. In end-user products, AI powers recommendation engines, chatbots, image recognition, and personalisation.

Key AI Concepts

TermDefinitionExample
Machine Learning (ML)AI systems that learn from data without explicit programmingSpam filters, recommendation engines
Deep LearningML using neural networks with many layersImage recognition, voice assistants
Natural Language ProcessingAI understanding and generating human languageChatGPT, Google Translate
Computer VisionAI interpreting images and videoFacial recognition, medical imaging
Generative AIAI that creates new content (text, images, code)GitHub Copilot, DALL-E, Claude
🏋️ Health & Fitness (OSP context)AI-powered personalised workout plan generation, injury risk prediction, form analysis via camera, intelligent calorie estimation from food photos
⚠️ Ethical concernsBias in training data, privacy of health data used for AI, explainability ("why did the AI recommend this?"), GDPR Article 22 (automated decision-making rights)
💼 Developer impactAI coding assistants increase productivity but may introduce security vulnerabilities. Developers must review AI-generated code critically.
🔮 Future directionAI agents that autonomously manage tasks, personalised AI tutors, real-time language translation, AI-generated synthetic training data

Internet of Things (IoT)

Physical devices connected to the internet, collecting and sharing data

📌 High Exam Relevance — Wearables

IoT devices have sensors, software, and network connectivity that allows them to collect and exchange data. There are estimated to be over 15 billion connected IoT devices globally. From smart watches to industrial sensors — all producing data that software systems must process.

⌚ Wearables (key for OSP)Fitness trackers (Fitbit, Apple Watch, Garmin) collect heart rate, steps, sleep, GPS data. A fitness app could integrate via APIs (Apple HealthKit, Google Fit) to auto-populate workout logs without manual entry.
🏠 Smart HomeSmart thermostats, security cameras, door locks all connected. Software must handle intermittent connectivity, device authentication, and encrypted data transmission.
🏥 Healthcare IoTRemote patient monitoring — blood glucose monitors, ECG patches transmitting data directly to doctors. Raises strict GDPR health data obligations.
⚠️ Security risksIoT devices are frequently poorly secured — default passwords, no encryption, infrequent firmware updates. A compromised IoT device can be used as a pivot point into a network.
⚠️
OSP relevance: In your proposal's wider context, mention wearable integration as an emerging technology opportunity for your fitness platform: "Future iterations could integrate with wearable APIs (Apple HealthKit, Google Fit) to automatically import workout data, removing manual entry friction and improving data accuracy."
🔗

Blockchain

Distributed, immutable ledger technology — beyond cryptocurrency

A blockchain is a chain of data blocks where each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, making it tamper-evident. Once data is written, it cannot be altered without changing all subsequent blocks — requiring consensus of the network.

Key Properties

🔒

Immutability

Once recorded, data cannot be altered without detection. Every block contains the hash of the previous block.

🌐

Decentralisation

No single point of control or failure. Copies exist across thousands of nodes globally.

👁️

Transparency

All transactions visible to network participants (public blockchains). Private blockchains restrict visibility.

💊 HealthcareImmutable patient records that patients control. Pharmaceutical supply chain verification — prevents counterfeit drugs.
📜 Digital credentialsVerifiable digital qualifications (e.g. T-Level certificates on a blockchain — tamper-proof, instantly verifiable by employers).
💰 CryptocurrencyBitcoin, Ethereum — decentralised digital currencies. Smart contracts automatically execute agreements when conditions are met.
⚠️ LimitationsHigh energy consumption (Proof of Work). Slow transaction speeds vs centralised databases. Irreversibility is a problem if errors are made.
🚁

Drones & Autonomous Systems

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) connected to software systems

Drones are software-controlled physical systems requiring: real-time data processing, GPS and sensor fusion, computer vision, and often AI for obstacle avoidance and route optimisation. They represent a convergence of multiple emerging technologies.

📦 LogisticsAmazon Prime Air drone delivery trials. Last-mile delivery automation reducing cost and carbon footprint.
🌾 AgriculturePrecision crop spraying, field surveying, yield mapping — drones replace manual surveying across thousands of acres.
🚨 Emergency servicesSearch and rescue, delivering medical supplies to remote areas, first responder cameras. NHS drone deliveries of blood between hospitals.
⚖️ RegulationCivil Aviation Authority (CAA) rules in the UK — drone operators need registration, follow airspace rules. Software must enforce geofencing to prevent no-fly zone violations.
💡
Software connection: Drone fleets require fleet management software, real-time telemetry dashboards, battery management systems, and AI-powered path planning algorithms — all areas directly relevant to software development skills.
📶

5G Networks

Fifth generation mobile network — ultra-fast, ultra-low latency connectivity

5G is not just faster 4G — it enables entirely new categories of applications that were previously impossible due to latency or bandwidth constraints.

Feature4G LTE5GWhat it enables
Peak speed~100 Mbps~10 GbpsInstant 4K video streaming, real-time cloud gaming
Latency~30–50ms<1msRemote surgery, real-time autonomous vehicle control
Device density~100k/km²~1M/km²Massive IoT deployments — smart cities
Reliability~99.9%~99.999%Mission-critical applications — industrial automation
🏥 Remote healthcare5G enables real-time remote surgery with haptic feedback robotic tools. Surgeons in London operating on patients in rural Scotland.
🚗 Autonomous vehiclesSelf-driving cars need <1ms latency to react to obstacles. V2X (vehicle-to-everything) communication for coordinated traffic flow.
🏋️ Fitness (OSP)Live HD video coaching streams, AR workout overlays, real-time biometric data from wearables processed in the cloud with instant feedback — all enabled by 5G's bandwidth and low latency.
⚠️ Security concernsVastly more connected devices = vastly larger attack surface. Network slicing (running multiple virtual networks on one physical 5G network) creates new isolation security challenges.
